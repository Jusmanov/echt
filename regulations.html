General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) - European Union

GDPR, implemented in 2018, is one of the most influential data protection regulations globally. It applies to EU citizens and organizations that handle their data. GDPR mandates strict rules for data processing, user consent, data breach notifications, and empowers individuals with rights to access, rectify, and erase their personal data.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) - United States

Enacted in 2020, CCPA aims to enhance consumer privacy rights for California residents. It grants users control over their personal information, enabling them to access, delete, and opt-out of the sale of their data. CCPA has implications for businesses that collect data from Californian consumers, even if those businesses are located outside California.

Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) - Canada

PIPEDA is Canada's federal privacy law governing the collection, use, and disclosure of personal information by private sector organizations. It requires organizations to obtain informed consent, handle data securely, and provide individuals with access to their personal information.

Brazilian General Data Protection Law (LGPD) - Brazil

Modeled after GDPR, LGPD came into effect in 2020 to regulate the processing of personal data in Brazil. It establishes principles for data processing, user rights, data protection officers, and data breach notification. LGPD applies to entities processing data in Brazil, regardless of their location.

Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) - Singapore

Singapore's PDPA safeguards personal data while balancing legitimate business interests. It outlines guidelines for data collection, consent, disclosure, and data protection obligations. PDPA empowers individuals with rights to access, correct, and withdraw consent for their data.
